# Defense Compliance, Standards & Org Structure

Research brief covering AS9100, MIL-STD, TRL, ITAR/EAR, CMMC, IV&V, and procurement cycles.

## Key Numbers

- AS9100 implementation: 4–9 months, $20–45K for 3 years (small shop)
- ITAR violations: up to $1.27M civil, $1M + 20 years criminal per violation
- CDRL labor: 20–40% of a program's labor goes to documentation deliverables
- SCIF construction: $500K–$2M+ for a small facility
- Clearance timelines: Interim Secret 5–30 days, full Secret ~138 days, TS 8–18 months
- CMMC Level 2 implementation: 6–12 months

## Org-Shaping Constraints

- QA must be organizationally independent from Engineering (AS9100, IV&V)
- FSO reports directly to CEO, not through engineering or operations (DCSA requirement)
- Empowered Official required for any ITAR-registered company
- IV&V requires 4 dimensions of independence: technical, managerial, financial, contractual
- CUI handling requires GCC High / GovCloud — not standard cloud

## Top 10 Pitfalls

1. Treating ITAR as "deal with later" — hiring foreign nationals without plan
2. Underestimating CDRL labor (20–40% of program cost)
3. Agile habits colliding with configuration management
4. AS9100 certificate without real working QMS
5. CUI on commercial IT (Gmail, standard Google Drive)
6. Confusing BD with sales (BD is relationship-driven, not transactional)
7. Running IV&V inside the dev team
8. Not sponsoring clearances early enough
9. Signing prime flowdown clauses without reading them
10. Not tracking CDRL as separate schedule
