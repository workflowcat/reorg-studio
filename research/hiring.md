# Hiring, Critical Roles, and Compensation for Defense Hardware

Research brief covering first hires, talent pipelines, compensation, and growth waves.

## Compensation Benchmarks (Anduril, Levels.fyi)

- Software Engineer: $207K (L3) → $517K+ (L7), median ~$268K TC
- Mechanical Engineer: $156K (L3) → $279K+ (L6), median ~$175K
- Manufacturing Engineer: $134K–$343K range
- Company median TC: ~$237K
- vs FAANG: within 10–20% at senior levels
- vs legacy primes: 20–50% premium

## Day-1 Critical Hires

| Day 1 | Month 3–6 | Wait |
|-------|-----------|------|
| CEO (mission face) | First mech engineer | Marketing |
| CTO / Head of Eng | First EE/firmware | Dedicated HR |
| Systems architect | First SW/autonomy | Dedicated finance |
| Ops/contracts lead | PM | Dedicated FSO |
| ITAR Empowered Official | Test engineer | Recruiter |

## Engineering Composition at ~20 People (14–15 engineers)

- Systems engineering: 1 (CTO)
- Mechanical: 2–3
- Electrical/power: 2
- Embedded/firmware: 1–2
- Software/autonomy: 2–3
- Test & integration: 1
- Manufacturing eng: 1

## Talent Pipelines

1. Military veterans / SkillBridge — high mission-fit, operator insight
2. Aerospace primes (Lockheed, RTX, Northrop) — MIL-STD discipline, often cleared
3. Government labs/FFRDCs (APL, MIT LL, MITRE) — deep tech, weaker product sense
4. Dual-use robotics (ex-Cruise, Waymo, Boston Dynamics) — autonomy talent

## Onboarding Reality

- Engineer ramp in defense hardware: 6 months to first meaningful contribution
- 12 months to subsystem ownership, 24 months to deep defense judgment
- "Defense buddy" pattern: 10+ year veteran paired with each new hire for 6 months
- Pre-clearance: put uncleared hires on unclassified (but ITAR-controlled) work

## Growth Hiring Waves

- ~30: first eng manager, PM, test engineer, supply chain person
- ~50: Head of Quality (independent), FSO, 2–3 PMs, first recruiter
- ~100+: VP Eng, CFO, security director, capture/proposal writers, GC
